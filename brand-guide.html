01 — Brand Overview

Who We Are

A guide for internal teams and external design contractors building within the Zoe Conference brand identity.

Zoe Conference is a Christian women's conference hosted by The Life Church in Memphis, TN. The name Zoe — drawn from the Greek word for life — reflects the transformative, Spirit-filled experience the conference creates for every attendee.

Designed for women in their 20s, Zoe centers on three pillars: powerful communication, authentic worship, and genuine community. The brand should always feel inviting, celebratory, and deeply intentional — rooted in faith while bold enough to capture a modern audience.

Visually, the Zoe identity draws from the tradition of illuminated manuscripts — the ornate, handcrafted beauty of historic Christian texts. Think antique gold, rich parchment, and the quiet feeling of something both ancient and completely alive.

Empowering

Every design choice should reinforce the intrinsic worth God has placed in every woman. Bold, confident, never timid.

Celebratory

Zoe is a gathering of joy. The brand should feel festive, warm, and full of life — not somber or overly formal.

Rooted

Grounded in Scripture and faith heritage. The illuminated manuscript aesthetic honors that history with modern intention.

04 — Visual Language

Aesthetic Direction

Zoe's visual identity is rooted in the illuminated manuscript tradition — honoring Christian heritage while feeling intentional, sophisticated, and deeply alive.

✦

Illuminated Manuscript

The primary visual reference. Ornate drop caps, decorative borders, gold leaf tones, botanical illustration. This aesthetic connects the modern conference to a centuries-long tradition of the written Word.

◈

Warm & Textured

Prefer textured, organic surfaces over flat digital finishes. Parchment, aged paper, subtle grain. Avoid sterile or overly polished aesthetics. The brand should feel handcrafted — made with care and intention.

❧

Ornamental Details

Corner flourishes, botanical vine motifs, diamond separators, thin ruled lines, and decorative borders are all on-brand. Use with restraint — each ornamental element should feel considered, never decorative for decoration's sake.

▣

Jewel Tone Accents

The Illumination Accent palette — forest green, medallion red, scripture blue — belongs only within illustration-style elements: decorative initials, botanical borders, manuscript-inspired art. Never in UI components.

✧

Photography Direction

Warm, golden-hour lighting. Intimate, candid moments of community and worship. Avoid stark, cold, or heavily filtered images. Photos should feel like they belong in the same warm, light-filled world as the brand palette.

◇

Spatial Intention

Don't overcrowd. Illuminated manuscripts used generous margins and deliberate white space — honor that in digital and print layouts. Give typography room to breathe. When in doubt, pull back and let the elements speak.

05 — Voice & Tone

How We Sound

Zoe copy is warm, empowering, celebratory, and faith-rooted. It speaks directly to women hungry for purpose — never down to them, always alongside them.

Empowering

We speak to the strength God has already placed inside every woman. Copy affirms, never diminishes. Use active voice, strong verbs, and language that calls women into something — not away from something.

Celebratory

Zoe is a party. Enthusiasm is on-brand. "Amazing communicators, incredible worship, and lots of fun" is not marketing hyperbole — it's the standard we write to. Let joy show up in the words.

Faith-Rooted

Scripture and Spirit-language are natural here, not forced. Reference God, worship, and community the way The Life Church does: accessible and alive, never stiff or jargon-heavy.

Direct & Warm

Short, clear sentences. Conversational but not careless. Write the way you'd talk to a friend who's ready to show up — honest, energetic, and genuinely inviting.

✓

God has placed something extraordinary inside you — and Zoe was built to help you find it.

Empowering, faith-rooted, direct.

✗

This event is for women who want to learn about their faith and attend a conference.

Flat, passive, no energy or invitation.

✓

Amazing communicators. Incredible worship. And honestly? A lot of fun.

Celebratory, punchy, authentic to the brand voice.

✗

We will be hosting speakers, a worship service, and activities for attendees.

Corporate-speak. No warmth, no heart.

✓

You were made for more. Come find out what more looks like.

Speaks directly to the reader. Invitational, not transactional.

✗

Women who attend will experience spiritual growth and community building opportunities.

Third-person, passive. Reads like a brochure, not a conversation.
